# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist

## General Safety Tips

- Always use strong and unique passwords for different accounts. Avoid using simple passwords like your name or date of birth.
- Do not share your personal information such as OTP, passwords, or bank details with anyone, even if they claim to be from a bank.
- Be careful while clicking on links received through SMS, email, or social media, as they may be fake or harmful.
- Keep your devices updated with the latest security updates and antivirus software.
- Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities like banking or online payments.
- Always log out from your accounts when using shared or public devices.

## UPI and Financial Safety Tips

- Never approve a payment request on UPI apps if you are expecting to receive money. Remember, receiving money does not require entering a UPI PIN.
- Always verify the name and details of the person before sending money through UPI.
- Do not scan unknown QR codes, as they may lead to unauthorized transactions.
- Set transaction limits on your banking apps to reduce risk in case of fraud.

## Reporting and Awareness

- In case of any cyber fraud, immediately report it on the National Cyber Crime Portal: https://www.cybercrime.gov.in
- You can also call the Cyber Crime Helpline Number: 1930 for quick assistance.
- Stay informed about common cyber scams and spread awareness among friends and family.

## Conclusion

Following these simple steps can help students stay safe in the digital world. Being alert and responsible while using the internet is the best way to prevent cybercrime.
